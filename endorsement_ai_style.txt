STOLEN WORDS: An Endorsement

In today's world, where authenticity and transparency matter more than ever, STOLEN WORDS emerges as a crucial investigation into one of the most significant yet underreported issues in spiritual publishing.

A Sacred Legacy at Stake

When A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupāda published his Bhagavad-gītā As It Is in 1972, he wasn't just creating another translation—he was offering a transformative gift to humanity. His vision was clear: preserve these teachings exactly as presented, unchanged for future generations. The establishment of the Bhaktivedanta Book Trust reflected this commitment to textual integrity.

However, after his passing in 1977, something deeply troubling occurred.

The Unauthorized Transformation

In 1983, a "revised and enlarged" edition appeared, containing thousands of changes that fundamentally altered the spiritual direction of the text. Here's what makes this particularly concerning:

• Prabhupāda had explicitly instructed: "Do not change my books"
• The revisions were made posthumously, without the author's consent
• Readers were never informed they weren't reading the original
• The changes systematically shifted the theological emphasis

This raises important questions about:
- Editorial authority vs. authorial intent
- Institutional responsibility to preserve sacred texts
- Reader rights to authentic source materials
- The ethics of posthumous revisions

A Journey of Discovery

STOLEN WORDS takes readers on a compelling investigative journey. Through careful analysis and engaging narrative storytelling, it reveals:

1. **The Scope of Changes**: Documented evidence of extensive textual alterations
2. **The Theological Shift**: How the revisions transformed grace-based devotion into knowledge-based practice
3. **The Impact on Readers**: Millions unknowingly following a different path than intended
4. **The Pattern of Silence**: Why transparency was avoided

Why This Matters

This isn't merely about one religious text or one tradition. The implications extend far beyond:

**For Spiritual Seekers**: The right to know what you're actually reading and following
**For Publishers**: The responsibility to maintain textual authenticity
**For Institutions**: The sacred duty to honor founder's explicit wishes
**For All Readers**: The principle of informed consent in spiritual matters

A Call for Transparency

At its heart, STOLEN WORDS advocates for something fundamental: honesty. Not demanding everyone choose one edition over another, but insisting that readers deserve to know:

✓ What the original author actually wrote
✓ What has been changed and why
✓ That they have a choice between different approaches

The book presents its case through:
- Rigorous textual comparison
- Verifiable documentation
- Engaging narrative structure
- Balanced, evidence-based analysis

Moving Forward

The question isn't whether well-intentioned people can disagree about editorial decisions. The question is: Why were readers never told? In an age where we value:

• Transparency in publishing
• Respect for authorial intent
• Consumer right to know
• Authentic spiritual guidance

...this silence becomes increasingly difficult to justify.

The Bottom Line

STOLEN WORDS doesn't tell you which path to follow. Instead, it:

→ Documents what occurred
→ Reveals the extent of changes
→ Advocates for reader awareness
→ Calls for institutional accountability

Whether you're a devoted practitioner, a spiritual seeker, a publishing professional, or simply someone who cares about textual integrity—this investigation offers crucial insights into how sacred texts can be transformed after their authors can no longer protect them.

The original teachings still exist. The evidence is verifiable. The choice can still be restored to readers.

But first, they need to know.

STOLEN WORDS ensures they know.

In a world hungry for authentic spiritual guidance, this book serves as both warning and opportunity—a reminder that preserving sacred wisdom requires not just devotion, but also unwavering commitment to truth and transparency.

Highly recommended for anyone who believes that spiritual seekers deserve access to authentic teachings, unchanged and clearly presented.
