AMAZON PRODUCT DESCRIPTION - STOLEN WORDS
Version 2.1 - October 21, 2025

========================================

Maya Chen's grandmother asked a simple question: "Why would they change his words?"

She'd been reading the Bhagavad-gita for forty years. Then she picked up a newer edition. Something felt wrong.

Maya compared them. Same book. Same translator. Thousands of words changed. After he died.

"Forgotten soul" became "forgetful soul." Someone decided the original wasn't good enough.

Maya's a neuroscientist. She knows words shape your brain during meditation. Change the words, change the experience.

She started investigating. California temples. Moscow prayer halls. Same pattern everywhere. Sacred phrases quietly replaced. Nobody told the readers.

Her grandmother's question haunted her. Why change words a teacher carefully chose? Words he approved while alive. Words millions trust.

This isn't about religion. It's about trust. When you open a sacred text, you assume what's inside is protected—not edited.

What if it's not? What if the version you're holding erased what you're searching for?

Some things, once seen, can't be unseen.
